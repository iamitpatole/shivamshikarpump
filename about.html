About Us

A mobile phone-powered weedicide spraying pump developed in 2011 by Ashok Patole from Vadner Bhairav, Nashik, has over the years become a sort of household name among cultivators across the state.

Ashok Patole was conferred upon the Aadarsh Shetkari Puraskar (Progressive Farmer Award) by the Agricultural Technology Management Agency (ATMA), Nashik for the pump. He was also be awarded with the National Unity Award during the fifth Rashtriya Ekta Gaurav Sammelan by Adarsh Foundation, a Kolhapur-based NGO.

It all started when Ashok Patole was facing labour issues in 2011. He kept trying his hand at easier means to spray his farm with weedicide. His experiments finally bore fruit when he tried using a mobile phone battery to supply power for the spraying pump. He patented his innovation and today supplies the sprayer to farmers across the state.

Technical Details

The Shivam Shikar Pump is invented as a solution to labour problems. Centrifugal force throws water in all directions.The device can water two acres of land with 10 litres of water, which would have otherwise taken 200 litres. Considering the labour charges, watering an acre costs Rs 4,000. This pump costs me only Rs 400 per acre. It weighs half a kg and is attached to a 10-litre water can.

This pump have a 6V motor at the lower end of a four-inch polyvinyl chloride pipe, four-feet long and half-inch wide attached with compact disk on the motor to revolve it. At the top end, there is socket to fix the mobile phone battery to operate the motor. Socket having an on and off switch for the power supply. we use 8-mm drip cock to regulate the flow of the weedicide solution. A 10-litre drum ready to mount on a user’s back is also provided to store the solution.

When pump is started, The solution is dropped on the Disk by a nozzle. A revolving disk and fine droplets spray the solution over a diameter of seven feet.

Why We are Different

Weed management is crucial and the labour problem is perennial. The present weedicide spray pumps offer a solution to labour problems but are costly. Shikar Pump can be operated effortlessly and efficiently.
